FADE IN:

INT. FAMILY DINING ROOM - EVENING

[Approx. Timestamp: 00:00:00]
A modest table set for three. Candles flicker. Soft jazz plays. SARAH (38, composed but strained) adjusts napkins. Her husband MARK (40, charming but distracted) pours wine. Their best friend since college, DAVID (39, warm smile), arrives with a bottle.

DAVID
Happy anniversary, you two. Ten years—still going strong.

They clink glasses. Laughter. Surface-level.

SARAH
We almost didn't make it to ten. Remember that rough patch in year seven?

MARK
(quickly)
Ancient history. We're good now.

David nods, but his eyes linger on Sarah a beat too long.

CUT TO:

INT. DINING ROOM - LATER

[Approx. Timestamp: 00:04:00]
Main course finished. Conversation turns personal.

DAVID
So, Mark... still traveling for work every month?

MARK
Yeah. Keeps the lights on.

SARAH
(quiet)
Keeps him away.

Awkward silence.

DAVID
You two okay?

MARK
We're fine. Just... life.

Sarah stands, clears plates. In the kitchen doorway, she pauses, overhears.

MARK (low, to David)
She doesn't know yet. About the promotion. Or... the rest.

DAVID
(whisper)
The rest?

MARK
New city. New start. I asked for the transfer. Alone.

David's face falls. He glances toward the kitchen—Sarah is frozen, listening.

SARAH
(soft, from doorway)
Alone?

Mark turns, startled.

MARK
Sarah... it's not—

SARAH
Not what? Not leaving me? Not after everything?

David stands.

DAVID
I should go.

SARAH
No. Stay. You knew, didn't you?

David looks down.

DAVID
He told me last week. I thought... he'd tell you first.

SARAH
(voice breaking)
You both decided this together? Like I'm not even here?

MARK
It wasn't like that. I was going to—

SARAH
Save it. Ten years. And you plan your escape with him.

She turns to David.

SARAH
And you... my friend. You let him. You comforted me when I cried about him being distant. You said "he's just stressed." All while knowing he was packing his life without me.

David reaches for her hand. She pulls away.

DAVID
Sarah, I—

SARAH
Don't. Just... don't.

Mark stands.

MARK
I love you. But I can't keep pretending this works.

SARAH
(quiet, cold)
Then go. Both of you.

She walks to the table, blows out the candles one by one. Darkness creeps in.

SARAH
(soft, to herself)
Happy anniversary.

She exits to the kitchen. Mark and David exchange a look—guilt, regret.

Mark grabs his coat. David follows.

The door closes behind them. Silence.

Sarah returns, sits alone at the dark table. A single tear falls.

FADE OUT.
